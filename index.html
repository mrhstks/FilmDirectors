In 1969, Wes Anderson was born in Houston Texas to a Ann and Melver Anderson.
He attended the Univeristy Of Texas, Austin where obtained his film degree
and befriended famous actor, Owen Wilson. His debut film, BottleRocket,
was the beginning of his impactful and groundbreaking film career. His numerous films
have generated several Academy and BAFTA awards, with his 2014,
The Grand Budapest Hotel, winnning the majority of them. His dynamic, clever,
and bold film style make him one of the most interesting filmmakers of all time.

In 1979, Jordena Peele was born in New York City to Lucinda Williams
Peele attended Sarah Lawerence College and recieved his first breakout role in 2003
on, MadTV. He started his career as an actor and comedian and made his tranistion to
filmmaker in 2017 with debut film Get Out. His pyscological and social thriller
films have turned the film word upside down. His films delve deep into the realities
of the human evils often seen in this world and challengs the common narratives.
His films have obtained much notoriety and several awards.

In 1958, Charlie Kaufman was born in New York City to Helen and Myron Kaufman.
He attended Boston Unverisity before eventually transferring to New York University
where he studied film. Most of the early part of career attempting to break into the industry.
However, in 1999 he became noticed when wrote, Being John Malkovich. Though he has done
some directorial work, most of his experience and heart lies with screenwriting. His stories have
been known to be a cerebrial adventure of the human mind that either leaves the audience
confused and frustrated or wanting more.

In 1972, Ava DuVernay was born in Long Beach, CA to Darlene and Joseph Duvernay.
She attended UCLA with a double major in,Literature English and African American Studies.
Her first film, I will Follow, debuted in 2011. Since then she has released several other well
dissected documentaries and narratives that have brought about numerous nominations
and awards. Most of her films explore the nature of history, truama, and
the marganilized experience. As an african-american women she has raised the bar
and cleared the path for many other directors to follow in her footsteps.
